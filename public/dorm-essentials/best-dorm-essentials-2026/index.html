Bedding: Start Here

Twin XL is non-negotiable. Standard twin sheets won't fit. Look for deep pockets (14-16") because dorm mattresses are often thicker than advertised with toppers.

Kitchen: Just the Basics

Most dorms have a communal kitchen down the hall. You need: microwave-safe bowl, mug, spoon, reusable water bottle. That's it. Skip the mini fridge if your hall has one — it's loud, expensive, and takes 20% of your floor space.

Buying Guide: What to Look For

Measure First

Check your dorm's mattress dimensions and closet width on the housing portal before buying. Some older dorms have shorter mattresses.

Coordinate with Roommate

Split costs on shared items: fridge, microwave, rug, cleaning supplies. Use a shared spreadsheet.

Command Strips > Nails

Every wall mount in this guide works with Command strips. Bring a variety pack (medium, large, outdoor).

Two Sets of Sheets

Laundry rooms are busy. Two sets means you're never sleeping on a bare mattress while one set washes.
