I moved into my first apartment at 22 with a futon, a hot plate, and zero clue. Three moves later, here's the checklist I wish I'd had — organized by room, priority, and budget. No fluff, just what you actually need.

Priority 1: Buy Before Move-In (Sleep, Eat, Shower)

These are non-negotiable. You need them day one.

  • Mattress + bed frame (or platform bed)
  • Bedding: sheets (2 sets), pillowcases, comforter, pillows (2)
  • Towels: bath (4), hand (4), washcloths (4)
  • Shower curtain + liner + rings
  • Toilet paper, hand soap, toothpaste
  • Basic cookware: 10" skillet, 2 qt saucepan, baking sheet
  • Basic utensils: chef's knife, cutting board, spatula, tongs, can opener
  • Plates/bowls (4 each), mugs (4), glasses (4)
  • Flatware set (service for 8)
  • Trash cans (kitchen, bath, bedroom) + bags
  • Light bulbs (check fixtures first)
  • WiFi router (don't rent from ISP)

Priority 2: First Week (Comfort & Function)

  • Instant Pot or slow cooker (meal prep game changer)
  • Coffee maker (French press = cheapest, best coffee)
  • Shower caddy (if no built-in niche)
  • Bath mat + rug
  • Command hooks (variety pack) — rental safe
  • Tool kit: hammer, screwdriver set, tape measure, level
  • First aid kit + basic meds
  • Cleaning supplies: all-purpose, glass, bathroom, vacuum/mop

Kitchen: The 80/20 Items

20% of kitchen tools handle 80% of cooking. Skip the unitaskers (avocado slicer, egg separator, banana guard).

  • Toilet paper holder (freestanding if no wall mount)

Buying Guide: What to Look For

Measure Doorways First

Queen mattress = 60" wide. Standard door = 32". Measure your hallway, stairwell, and bedroom door before ordering. Some mattresses ship compressed (Tuft & Needle does) — solves this.

Buy Used: Furniture, Not Bedding

Facebook Marketplace / Craigslist for: dressers, nightstands, dining tables, bookshelves. Never used: mattress, pillows, underwear, towels.

Renter's Insurance = $15/mo

Covers your stuff (electronics, clothes, furniture) against theft, fire, water damage. Required by most leases anyway. Lemonade, State Farm, Geico all offer it.
