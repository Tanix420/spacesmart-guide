My first studio had a two-burner electric stove that took 20 minutes to boil water. I've since tested every category of compact appliance in real tiny kitchens (150-400 sq ft). The rule: if it doesn't replace at least two things, it doesn't get counter space.

Coffee: Skip the Machine, Get a Scale

Drip machines take counter space, make mediocre coffee, grow mold. For tiny kitchens: AeroPress + Fellow Stagg EKG kettle + cheap burr grinder = café quality in 6" x 6" footprint.

What We'd Skip

  • Mini fridge (unless no fridge at all) — loud, inefficient, tiny capacity
  • Compact dishwasher — 18" wide, still needs plumbing, hand-washing is faster for 1-2 people
  • Rice cooker — Instant Pot or pot on induction burner does it better
  • Blender — immersion blender (6" tall) replaces full blender for soups, smoothies, sauces

Buying Guide: What to Look For

Measure Your Counter Depth

Standard = 24". Many compact appliances are 12-14" deep. Leave 4" clearance behind for cords/ventilation.

Check Your Electrical

Older buildings: 15A circuits. 1800W induction + 1500W air fryer = trip risk. Run one high-watt appliance at a time.

Ventilation Matters

Air fryers and toaster ovens pump heat. Place near window or under range hood. Never under cabinets without 6" clearance.
